About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Fellow at the Auckland Bioengineering Institute, The University of Auckland. My research sits at the intersection of artificial intelligence, medical robotics, and biomedical engineering.

I received my Ph.D. in Control Science and Engineering from Tongji University in 2025. My work develops AI systems for 3D spatial understanding, robotic perception, embodied intelligence, and healthcare applications.

Research Interest

My research focuses on embodied AI, medical robotics, multimodal perception, causal inference, and human-robot interaction. I am especially interested in intelligent systems that can perceive, reason, and act safely in complex physical and clinical environments.

Current projects include soft tissue segmentation, deformation tracking, force-augmented visual tracking, and mixed-reality surgical guidance for healthcare platforms.
